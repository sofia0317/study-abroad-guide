Budget snapshot for Sydney

Sydney is beautiful but not cheap, especially rent and eating out. With shared housing, a student Opal card and a mix of cooking and café trips, many students report spending around A$2,000 per month.

Example monthly budget:

  • Rent (shared flat): ~A$1,150
  • Groceries & coffee: ~A$350
  • Opal card & transport: ~A$120
  • Going out & beach days: ~A$260
  • Misc / buffer: ~A$120

Why study in Sydney?

Sydney gives you a big-city campus life with beaches, coastal walks and ferries built into your weekday routine. You might have lecture in the morning, grab coffee in a laneway café, and end the day watching the sunset at the harbour.

It’s active, outdoorsy and very café-based, but the spread-out layout means you’ll think a lot about commute times and which neighborhood to call home. This guide is here to help you picture what daily life and costs look like.

Neighborhoods to know

Different programs use different campuses, but these areas show up a lot for students.

Newtown

Student-heavy · Street art · Veggie food · $$

Close to the University of Sydney, full of second-hand shops, cheap eats and live music. Great if you like a slightly grungy, creative vibe and don’t mind a bit of late-night noise.

Surry Hills

Cafés · Brunch spots · Terraces · $$$

Known for brunch, small bars and pretty terrace houses. Walkable to the CBD and popular with young professionals, so rents are higher but the “everything is nearby” factor is strong.

Manly

Beach town · Ferry commute · Surf vibe · $$

A beach suburb across the harbour with surf, coastal walks and a slower pace. Your commute might include a ferry ride, which is gorgeous but adds time—worth it if you’re a beach person.

Quick tips for students

  • Get an Opal card early; tapping on/off buses, trains and ferries becomes automatic very fast.
  • Cafés are a big part of social life, “grabbing a coffee” is basically a hobby, so budget a little for it.
  • Australia’s seasons are flipped from the northern hemisphere; February can feel like peak summer.
  • Sun is intense. Sunscreen, a hat and reusable water bottle are not optional if you’re outside a lot.
