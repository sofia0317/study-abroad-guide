Budget snapshot for Berlin

Berlin used to be known as “cheap,” but prices have risen—especially rent. Still, food and culture can be more affordable than in some other capitals. Many students estimate around €1,200 per month including shared rent, groceries, BVG pass and going out.

Example monthly budget:

  • Rent (WG / shared flat): ~€650
  • Groceries & coffee: ~€230
  • BVG pass (U-Bahn, S-Bahn, buses): ~€70–80
  • Going out & culture: ~€170
  • Misc / buffer: ~€70

Why study in Berlin?

Berlin is a mix of history, street art, canals, smoky cafés and long nights that sometimes end at sunrise. It’s packed with museums, galleries and green spaces, but also has a very relaxed “do your thing” energy.

As a student you’ll juggle classes, language practice, late tram rides and endless new neighborhoods to explore. This guide focuses on what daily life might actually feel like, not just techno and the Brandenburg Gate.

Neighborhoods to know

Most students end up in WGs (shared flats). These boroughs come up a lot.

Kreuzberg

Street art · Bars · Food · $$

Lively and diverse with lots of bars, falafel spots and canal walks. Great nightlife and food, but some streets can stay loud late into the night.

Prenzlauer Berg

Cafés · Families · Leafy streets · $$$

Calmer and more polished, with lots of cafés, brunch spots and pretty side streets. Feels safer and quieter, but rent tends to be higher and it’s more residential.

Neukölln

Multicultural · Nightlife · Parks · $$

A mix of laid-back bars, international food and green spaces like Tempelhofer Feld. Still relatively affordable by Berlin standards, with a strong student and artist presence.

Quick tips for students

  • Get a semester ticket or student BVG pass if your program offers one, it makes public transport basically unlimited.
  • Cash and card are both common, but some smaller places are still “cash only,” so keep a little on you.
  • Many bars and clubs run late; plan your transport home, especially if you’re far from an S-Bahn or U-Bahn line.
  • Learning some basic German phrases goes a long way, even though many people speak English in the city.
