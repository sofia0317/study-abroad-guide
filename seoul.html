Budget snapshot for Seoul

Why study in Seoul?

Seoul is a city where you can grab street food at midnight, study in a café until 2am, and still catch a late subway home. As a student, your daily life blends campus, convenience stores, riverside walks and neon neighborhoods.

It’s fast-paced and hyper-connected, but there are also quiet hills, palaces and river paths where you can unplug. This guide aims to give you a realistic sense of the rhythm, costs and neighborhoods before you land.

Neighborhoods to know

These aren’t the only choices, but they’re common bases for international and exchange students.

Hongdae

Student area · Nightlife · Street performers · $$

Packed with cafés, bars, live music and clothing shops. Great if you want to be surrounded by other students and don’t mind noise on weekends. Housing can be a bit pricier because it’s so popular.

Gangnam

Modern · High-rise · Shopping · $$$

Sleek office towers, big malls and late-night restaurants. More expensive, but convenient if your campus or internships are on the south side of the river and you like a polished vibe.

Sinchon & Ewha

Campus cluster · Cafés · Shopping streets · $$

Close to several major universities with tons of study cafés, cosmetic shops and affordable restaurants. Good balance between student life and convenience, with easy access to the subway.

Quick tips for students

  • Get a T-money or Cashbee card on day one; you’ll use it for subways, buses and even some convenience stores.
  • Most cafés expect quiet studying, but many offer outlets and Wi-Fi with no time limit as long as you order something.
  • Food portions can be big, sharing dishes at Korean restaurants is normal and can save money.
  • Winters are cold and summers are humid. Pack layers and a good coat if you’re there in January or February.
