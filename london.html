Budget snapshot for London

London is notorious for rent and transport costs, but student discounts and shared flats help. Many exchange students report spending around £1,400 per month including housing, food, an Oyster card and going out.

Example monthly budget:

  • Rent (shared flat / hall): ~£800
  • Groceries & coffee: ~£250
  • Transport (Oyster / travelcard): ~£150
  • Going out & culture: ~£140
  • Misc / buffer: ~£60

Why study in London?

London gives you world-class museums, huge parks and dozens of different neighborhoods that all feel like mini-cities. Your commute might be a mix of Tube, buses and long walks through rows of townhouses.

It’s busy and fast, but there’s always a quiet square, café or park bench if you know where to look. This guide is here to help you picture a realistic version of London student life and not just the postcards.

Neighborhoods to know

Where you live will depend on your campus, but these areas are common student bases.

Shoreditch

Street art · Nightlife · Start-up vibe · $$$

Lots of bars, coffee shops and creative offices. Great if you like busy nights out and a trendier feel, but rents and noise can be intense, especially around weekends.

Brixton

Markets · Music · Diverse food · $$

A lively, multicultural area with food markets, live music and good bus and Tube links. More affordable than some zones, but definitely not quiet.

Notting Hill

Pastel houses · Portobello Road · Parks · $$$

Leafy, photogenic streets and easy access to Hyde Park and Kensington. Quieter than central nightlife areas and very pretty, but that shows up in the rent prices.

Quick tips for students

  • Get an Oyster card or link a contactless card to benefit from daily and weekly “caps” on fares.
  • Museums like the Tate, V&A and British Museum are free—perfect for rainy-day studying or wandering.
  • Plan extra travel time. Changing lines on the Tube or waiting for buses can take longer than maps suggest.
  • Weather changes fast: a small umbrella and waterproof shoes will save you more than once.
